02 — My Story

From the lab bench to ecosystem building.

"I did not come into biotech through finance. I came into it through the lab."

My path started early. Long before I understood venture capital, I understood what disease could take from a family. That experience made biology personal for me — it pushed me into laboratories and later into stem cell science, where I became obsessed with one question: can we move medicine from treatment toward repair?

I started in stem cell labs in New York — at Mount Sinai, Weill Cornell, Vanderbilt, and the New York Stem Cell Foundation — developing iPSC protocols that generated a patent, a publication, and disease models that led to clinical trials for Giant Axonal Neuropathy.

Qatar came next. As the youngest scientist appointed to establish Qatar's first national stem cell core facility under Qatar Foundation and Sidra Medicine, I learned what it means to build scientific infrastructure in a place determined to build from scratch.

Oxford sharpened everything. As Sir Magdi Yacoub Fellow — funded through a special award from H.H. Sheikha Moza bint Nasr — I completed a DPhil in cardiovascular pharmacology and stem cell biology. Two Pak1 agonist candidates from that work are now in clinical studies.

Over time the question became larger. The bottleneck was not only discovery — it was translation. Who funds the science, structures the company, protects the IP, guides the regulatory path, and builds the ecosystem. That realization took me into venture capital, and has not let go since.

05 — Sovereign & Ecosystem

The countries that lead the next era of healthcare will not simply import medicines. They will build the infrastructure to discover, finance, manufacture, test, and deploy them.

National biomedical advantage is not an accident. It is built — through deliberate investment in talent, infrastructure, capital, governance, and the global partnerships that allow local science to reach global markets.

What sovereign partners need to build enduring biomedical advantage.

I have spent time working inside Qatar's early stem cell infrastructure, advising GCC sovereign investors, and building investment platforms across the U.S., Europe, and the Gulf.

01A credible scientific thesis — rooted in biology most tractable and most likely to produce durable advantage.
02A fund or investment platform — to deploy capital systematically with governance structures institutional LPs trust.
03Clinical and translational infrastructure — hospitals, patient registries, and clinical trial capabilities that attract global science.
04Academic and research partnerships — with Oxford, Harvard, MIT, Karolinska, and others that create the intellectual engine for discovery.
05Company creation capability — the ability to build ventures around local science, not only invest into foreign ones.
06Talent attraction and retention — an ecosystem attractive enough that the world's best scientists choose to build here.
07Global venture and pharma connectivity — so local companies can access the syndicates and markets that determine commercial success.
08Governance that protects capital and reputation — because institutional credibility, once lost, is almost impossible to rebuild.

My Role

I help bridge the worlds that rarely speak fluently to each other: scientists, clinicians, venture capitalists, sovereign investors, universities, hospitals, founders, and strategic partners. I have sat at every one of those tables. That fluency is rare — and it is what allows me to translate between ambition and execution.
